Ultimate Guide to Creating Free Custom Text Emotes for Twitch

Not every streamer has the budget to hire a professional digital artist for custom drawn emotes right away. Fortunately, Text Emotes (like L, W, GG, RIP, HYPE, F) are incredibly popular on Twitch, YouTube, and Discord because they are highly readable even at the smallest 28x28 pixel size.

Our Free Twitch Emote Text Maker allows you to instantly generate bold, stylized text emotes without needing Photoshop. Choose from streamer-favorite fonts like Bangers or Anton, pick a bright fill color, add a thick outline stroke for maximum readability against dark backgrounds, and instantly download your transparent PNG emotes in all required Twitch sizes.

How to upload text emotes to Twitch:

  1. Go to your Twitch Creator Dashboard.
  2. Navigate to Viewer Rewards -> Emotes.
  3. If you have "Auto-Resize" enabled, simply upload the 112x112 PNG generated by our tool. Twitch will handle the rest.
  4. If you have it disabled, upload all three sizes (112x112, 56x56, 28x28) provided by our generator.
  5. Name your emote (e.g., PrefixHYPE) and submit!

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use these emotes on Discord or YouTube?

Yes! Discord and YouTube both accept transparent PNG files for custom server emojis and channel memberships. The 112x112 pixel size is perfect for both platforms and will scale down beautifully.

What makes a good text emote?

A good text emote must be easily readable at very small sizes. Keep the text short (1 to 4 characters like "GG" or "W"). Use a thick, bold font, and always include a contrasting outline (stroke) so the text stands out whether the viewer is using Light Mode or Dark Mode.
