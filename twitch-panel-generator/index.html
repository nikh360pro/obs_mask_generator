The Ultimate Guide to Twitch Panels (2025 Edition)

Your Twitch channel panels are the first thing viewers see when they scroll down your profile. They aren't just decorative images; they are powerful tools to drive traffic, explain your schedule, and monetize your stream. A professional panel setup signals to sponsors and viewers that you take content creation seriously.

🚀 Why You Need Custom Panels

Default text panels look amateurish. Custom panels allow you to:

  • Establish Branding: Use your channel's color palette (e.g., Cyberpunk Neon or Cozy Pastels) to create a cohesive look.
  • Direct Traffic: Visual buttons for "Donate" or "Discord" get 3x more clicks than plain text links.
  • Convey Info Quickly: Viewers scan profiles. Icons help them identify "Rules" or "Specs" instantly.

Twitch Panel Size & Format Guide

Getting the dimensions right is critical. If your image is too wide, Twitch will scale it down, causing blurriness. If it's too small, it will look pixelated.

The Golden Dimensions:

  • Width: Exactly 320 pixels. This is the hard limit set by Twitch.
  • Height: Flexible, but we recommend 70px - 150px.
  • File Size: Under 2.9 MB (Our tool optimizes this automatically).
  • Format: PNG (for transparent backgrounds) or GIF (for animations).

Design Tips for High-CTR Panels

Want more clicks on your donation or Discord links? Follow these design rules:

  1. High Contrast Text: Ensure your text stands out against the background. Dark text on light backgrounds or white text on dark backgrounds works best.
  2. Simple Icons: Use the "Icon" tab in our generator to add visual cues (e.g., a Gamepad for 'Games', a Calendar for 'Schedule').
  3. Consistent Theme: Don't mix styles. If you use the Cyberpunk theme for your 'About' panel, use it for your 'Donate' panel too.
  4. Rounded Corners: Use the "Corner Radius" slider to soften the edges. 10px-15px radius is the modern standard (used by UI designs like Discord and Spotify).

How to Install Your New Panels

Once you've downloaded your panels using the "Download PNG" button above, follow these steps:

  1. Go to your Twitch Channel Page (twitch.tv/yourname).
  2. Click the "About" tab.
  3. Toggle the "Edit Panels" switch to ON.
  4. Click the huge "+" button box.
  5. Select "Add a Text or Image Panel".
  6. Click "Add Image" and upload your PNG/GIF.
  7. Crucial Step: Add the destination arrow link (URL) where you want the panel to go (e.g., your Discord invite link).
  8. Click "Submit" to save.

⚠️ Troubleshooting Common Issues

1. My panel looks blurry on Twitch!

This happens if your image wasn't 320px wide or if you saved it as a low-quality JPEG. Always use our "Download PNG" button, which ensures pixel-perfect 320px width.

2. The GIF isn't animating.

Twitch sometimes freezes GIFs if the file size is too huge. Ensure you keep the "Animation Speed" to standard and avoiding complex gradients if you are hitting file size limits.

3. White background appearing behind rounded corners?

This means you saved as JPEG, which doesn't support transparency. Use PNG format to keep those corners smooth and transparent.
