Why 6000 Kbps?

6000 Kbps is Twitch's maximum bitrate for affiliates and most partners. Going higher risks buffering for viewers without transcoding access. This bitrate provides a BPP (Bits Per Pixel) of ~0.08, which is considered "good" for fast-motion content.

At 6000 Kbps, 1080p 60fps looks excellent for most games. Fast-paced shooters may show some compression in chaotic scenes, but it's generally imperceptible.

Twitch Partner Benefits

Twitch Partners can request up to 8500 Kbps by contacting Twitch support. This provides even better quality but requires guaranteed transcoding to avoid viewer buffering.

FAQ

What bitrate for Twitch 1080p 60fps?

6000 Kbps (CBR) is the standard and maximum for affiliates. Partners can go up to 8500 Kbps.

Can I use higher than 6000 on Twitch?

Only partners with transcoding guarantee. Affiliates streaming above 6000 Kbps risk viewer buffering.

H.264 or AV1 for Twitch?

Use H.264. Twitch's AV1 support is in beta and not widely available yet.
