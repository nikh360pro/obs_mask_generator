Fix OBS Audio Delay & Sync Issues

Quick Answer: Click the Gear Icon in the Audio Mixer > Advanced Audio Properties. Use the Sync Offset (ms) column. If audio is early (heard before seen), add a positive number (e.g., 200ms). If audio is late, use a negative number.

Why Does Desync Happen?

Webcams and Capture Cards have latency (processing time). Your microphone is usually instant. This means your voice arrives 200-500ms before your face moves on screen. This "Mouth moves later than voice" effect is extremely distracting for viewers.

The Clap Test Method

  1. Start a Local Recording in OBS (don't stream yet).
  2. Clap your hands loudly in front of the camera. Do this 3-4 times clearly.
  3. Stop recording and watch the video in a player like VLC.
  4. Pause exactly when you hear the sound. Is your hands touching yet?
  5. Guestimate the delay (e.g., "sound was half a second early").

Applying the Fix

  1. Go to Advanced Audio Properties in OBS.
  2. Find your Mic/Aux.
  3. Set Sync Offset to 200ms (start here).
  4. Record another test. If it's better but not perfect, try 300ms.
  5. Repeat until the clap sound and visual clash perfectly.
