Fix OBS "Encoding Overloaded" Error

Updated January 2026 • Troubleshooting Guide

⚡ Quick Fix

Fastest solution: Switch encoder from x264 (CPU) to NVENC (GPU). Go to Settings → Output → Encoder → select "NVIDIA NVENC H.264". This offloads encoding to your GPU.

Why "Encoding Overloaded" Happens

This error means your computer can't encode video frames fast enough. OBS is dropping frames because the encoder (CPU or GPU) is overwhelmed. Common causes:

  • Using x264 (CPU) encoder while gaming
  • Encoder preset too slow for your hardware
  • Resolution or FPS too high
  • Background apps using resources

Step-by-Step Fixes

Fix 1: Switch to NVENC (GPU Encoding)

Settings → Output → Encoder → NVIDIA NVENC H.264. This uses your GPU's dedicated encoder, freeing your CPU.

Fix 2: Lower Encoder Preset

For NVENC: Change from P7 to P5 or P6. For x264: Change from "slow" to "veryfast".

Fix 3: Reduce Output Resolution

Settings → Video → Output Resolution → 1280x720 instead of 1080p. Lower resolution = less encoding work.

Fix 4: Lower FPS

Change from 60fps to 30fps. Half the frames = half the encoding load.

Fix 5: Close Background Apps

Close browsers, Discord overlay, game launchers. Check Task Manager for CPU-heavy processes.

FAQ

Why does OBS say encoding overloaded?

Your CPU or GPU can't encode frames fast enough. The encoder is overwhelmed by the workload.

How do I fix encoding overloaded?

Switch to NVENC, lower your preset (P5-P6), reduce resolution, or close background apps.
