SOLVED: Why is my Webcam Blurry in OBS?

Quick Answer: Most blurry cams in OBS happen because the default "Device Default" setting uses a low resolution (like 640x480). To fix it, go to Source Properties > Change Resolution/FPS Type to Custom > Select 1920x1080.

Step 1: The Resolution Bug

By default, OBS tries to pick a "safe" resolution for your webcam to prevent lag. Often, this is 480p or even 360p. Even if you have a 4K Logitech Brio, OBS might be downscaling it.

  1. Right-click your Video Capture Device in OBS.
  2. Select Properties.
  3. Find Resolution/FPS Type. It usually says "Device Default".
  4. Switch it to Custom.
  5. In the Resolution dropdown, manually pick the highest number (e.g., 1920x1080).

Step 2: Check Your Bitrate

If your camera looks good in the preview but blurry on stream, your Streaming Bitrate is too low.

  • 720p 60fps: Needs at least 4,500 Kbps.
  • 1080p 60fps: Needs at least 6,000 Kbps.

If you try to stream 1080p at 2,000 Kbps, the encoder will destroy your quality to make it fit, resulting in a blocky, blurry mess.

Step 3: Lighting is Key

A $3,000 camera looks like trash in the dark. A $50 webcam looks amazing with good light. Webcams increase "ISO" (digital gain) in low light, which adds noise/grain. More light = lower ISO = cleaner image.
