Best OBS Filters for Blue Yeti

Quick Answer: The Blue Yeti is very sensitive and picks up keyboard clicks. You need a Noise Gate (-32dB) and Noise Suppression (RNNoise) to silence the background.

Step 1: Proper Gain

Before adding filters, turn the Gain Knob on the back of the Yeti down to about 25-30%. If it's too high, it captures room echo.

The Perfect Filter Chain

Add these filters in this exact order (Right-click Mic > Filters):

  1. Noise Suppression: Method: RNNoise (Good Quality).
  2. Noise Gate: Close Threshold: -32dB, Open Threshold: -26dB.
  3. Compressor: Ratio: 4:1, Threshold: -18dB. (Makes loud screams quieter).
  4. Limiter: Threshold: -3dB. (Prevents peaking/distortion).

Audio Out of Sync?

Adding filters takes processing time (latency). Check if your mic is still synced with your camera.
