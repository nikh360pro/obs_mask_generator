Understanding Bitrate Basics

What is Bitrate?

Bitrate measures how much data is transmitted per second, measured in Kbps (kilobits per second). Higher bitrate = more data = sharper image, but also requires more upload bandwidth.

The Bitrate Formula

A rough guideline for calculating bitrate:

Bitrate = Width √ó Height √ó FPS √ó 0.1 √∑ 1000

For 1080p 60fps: 1920 √ó 1080 √ó 60 √ó 0.1 √∑ 1000 = 12,442 Kbps

But Twitch caps at 6,000, which is why 1080p60 on Twitch can look blurry during fast motion.

Common Bitrate Mistakes

  1. Setting bitrate too high - Causes dropped frames and buffering
  2. Setting bitrate too low - Causes blocky, pixelated video
  3. Ignoring your upload speed - Your bitrate can't exceed your upload capacity
  4. Using CBR for recording - Use CRF/CQP for local recordings, CBR for streaming

FAQ

Is higher bitrate always better?

No. Higher bitrate only helps if your upload can handle it. Too high causes buffering for viewers and dropped frames for you.

Why does my 1080p stream look worse than 720p?

At Twitch's 6,000 Kbps cap, 1080p doesn't have enough data per pixel. 720p at 6,000 Kbps actually looks sharper than 1080p at the same bitrate.

What encoder should I use?

NVENC (NVIDIA) is best for most streamers - minimal performance impact. x264 gives better quality but uses more CPU. Use NVENC unless your GPU is weak.
