How to Add Overlays to OBS

  1. Open OBS Studio
  2. In Sources, click + ‚Üí Image
  3. Name it (e.g., "Webcam Frame")
  4. Browse and select your PNG overlay
  5. Position it over your webcam or content
  6. Right-click ‚Üí Transform ‚Üí Fit to screen if needed

üí° Layer Order Matters

In OBS, sources at the top of the list appear on top visually. Put your overlay above your webcam in the sources list.

Design Tips

  • Keep it simple - Don't cover important gameplay areas
  • Match your brand - Use consistent colors across all overlays
  • Use transparency - PNG format with transparent backgrounds
  • Don't obstruct gameplay - Keep overlays to edges/corners
  • Test at different sizes - Check how it looks on mobile viewers

FAQ

What format should overlays be?

PNG with transparency. JPEG doesn't support transparency, so you'd have an ugly white box.

Do overlays affect stream performance?

Static PNG overlays have zero impact. Animated overlays (GIF/WebM) use slightly more resources.

Can I use animated overlays?

Yes! Use a Media Source instead of Image Source in OBS. Supports GIF, WebM, and MP4.
