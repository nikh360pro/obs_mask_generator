Pro Tips for Professional Presentation Videos

1. Optimize Presenter Positioning

Position Best For Size
Bottom-Right Corner Standard presentations, lectures 20-30% of screen
Bottom-Left Corner Right-to-left language slides (Arabic, Hebrew) 20-30% of screen
Side Panel (Left/Right) Webinars, panel discussions 30-40% of width
Center (Slides in Background) Presenter-focused content, keynotes 50-60% of screen

Rule of thumb: If slides contain critical text/data, presenter = 20-25%. If presenter is primary focus, increase to 40-50%.

2. Add Captions for Accessibility

Many platforms require captions (ADA compliance in US, accessibility laws globally):

  • Auto-generate: YouTube auto-captions (free, then edit for accuracy)
  • Professional: Rev.com ($1.25/min) or Otter.ai (free tier)
  • Video editor: DaVinci Resolve has subtitle track, CapCut has Auto Captions

3. Create Slide Templates for Consistency

Designate a "presenter zone" in your slide template:

  1. PowerPoint/Google Slides ‚Üí Slide Master view
  2. Add a shape (rectangle) in bottom-right corner (e.g., 400x300px)
  3. Set shape fill to solid color (use for reference, delete before export)
  4. Ensure all slide content stays outside this zone
  5. Save as template

This prevents text/images from being blocked by your presenter video.

Common Challenges & Solutions

Challenge 1: Presenter Video Too Large (Blocks Slide Content)

Solution: Resize presenter to maximum 30% of screen. If slide has critical content everywhere, split into 2 slides or use side-panel layout instead of corner.

Challenge 2: Audio Out of Sync with Slides

Solution: In video editor, use audio waveform to align presenter speech with slide transitions. Add slight crossfade (0.5 seconds) for smooth transitions.

Challenge 3: Presenter Movements Are Distracting

Solution: When filming, minimize hand movements and keep upper body relatively still. If necessary, crop presenter video tighter (head and shoulders only) to reduce visible movement.

Challenge 4: File Size Too Large for LMS Upload

Solution: Export at 720p instead of 1080p (usually acceptable for e-learning), or use H.265 codec for 50% smaller files at same quality (newer LMS platforms support it).

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a green screen to create presenter-on-slides videos?

No! The free AI background remover works with any background. Film yourself on any background, upload, and AI removes it automatically. No green screen equipment needed.

Can I use this for pre-recorded webinars?

Absolutely! If you recorded a webinar where you presented slides, extract the presenter video, remove the background, and re-overlay on clean slides for a polished final version. Great for repurposing live content.

What if my slides have animation? Will presenter video block it?

Position presenter in a corner or side panel where animations don't occur. Alternatively, time presenter video to appear/disappear between animated sections using video editor layer visibility.

How do I handle long presentations (30+ minutes)?

Break into chapters (5-10 minute segments). Remove background from each chapter, then combine in video editor. This also makes content more digestible for viewers (higher completion rates).

Can I add my company logo/branding to the presenter video?

Yes! After removing background, import into video editor and add logo watermark in corner. Or better yet, include logo directly on your slide template for consistent branding across entire presentation.
