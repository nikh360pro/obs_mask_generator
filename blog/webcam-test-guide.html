Webcam Test Guide: 5 Steps to Better Lighting & Framing

January 22, 2026 • 5 min read

1. Turn Off Auto-Exposure (The #1 Fix)

Webcams try to brighten dark rooms by slowing down the shutter speed. This causes a "ghosting" or "smearing" effect when you move your hands.

The Fix:

  1. Open your webcam settings (In OBS: Right-click Source > Properties > Configure Video).
  2. Find "Exposure".
  3. Uncheck "Auto".
  4. Lower the exposure slider until the video is darker (and smoother).
  5. Compensate by improving your room lighting.

2. Lighting: The 3-Point Setup

Never stream with a window behind you (backlighting). It turns you into a silhouette.

  • Key Light: The main light. Place it 45 degrees to your side.
  • Fill Light: A softer light on the other side to fill in shadows.
  • Back Light (Hair Light): A colored LED behind you to separate you from the background.

3. Framing: The Rule of Thirds

Don't put your face in the dead center of the frame with a foot of empty space above your head.

✅ Correct Framing

Your eyes should be about 2/3rds up the screen. Leave only a tiny gap between the top of your head and the top of the frame.

4. White Balance

If you look like a Smurf (blue tint) or an Oompa Loompa (orange tint), your White Balance is wrong. Uncheck "Auto White Balance" and adjust the slider until your skin tone looks natural compared to a white sheet of paper.

5. Cleaning the Lens

It sounds stupid, but 50% of "blurry" webcams are just dirty fingerprints. Wipe your lens with a microfiber cloth before every stream.
