Streamlabs Desktop vs OBS Studio: Which is Best in 2026?

January 22, 2026 • 7 min read

It is the oldest debate in streaming. Do you go with the open-source powerhouse (OBS Studio) or the polished, user-friendly package (Streamlabs)? In 2026, the gap has narrowed, but the choice is clearer than ever depending on what kind of streamer you are.

🏆 Quick Verdict

  • Choose OBS Studio if: You want maximum performance, zero bloat, customization (plugins), and free features.
  • Choose Streamlabs if: You are a complete beginner who wants a "one-click" setup with chat/alerts built-in and don't mind higher CPU usage.

1. Performance (CPU & Ram Usage)

Winner: OBS Studio

This is not a close contest. OBS Studio is bare-bones and efficient. Streamlabs Desktop is essentially OBS wrapped in a web browser (Electron app) to show alerts and chat. This wrapper consumes significantly more RAM and CPU.

  • OBS Studio: Uses ~2-5% CPU on idle.
  • Streamlabs: Uses ~10-15% CPU on idle (varies by setup).

If you have a budget PC, Streamlabs might cause your game to lag.

2. Features & Plugins

Winner: OBS Studio

OBS Studio supports third-party plugins like StreamFX (blur, shaders) and Multi-RTMP (stream to Twitch + YT simultaneously). The community creates incredible tools everyday.

Streamlabs is a "Walled Garden." You generally can only use features they build. While they have an App Store, much of it is paid (Prime).

3. Ease of Use

Winner: Streamlabs Desktop

Streamlabs shines here. When you install it, it scans your internet speed, optimizes settings, and imports your alerts automatically. It has a dashboard built-in so you can change stream titles without opening a browser.

OBS requires you to manually add browser sources for alerts and chat, which can be intimidating for day-one streamers.

4. The Cost (Free vs Paid)

Feature OBS Studio Streamlabs
Software Price Free (Open Source) Free (Basic)
Multistreaming Free (via Plugin) Paid (Prime Sub)
Overlay Themes Free Community Assets Paid (Prime Sub)
Custom Branding Unlimited limited on free tier

Streamlabs pushes their "Ultra" (formerly Prime) subscription heavily. OBS Studio asks for nothing.

Conclusion: Making the Switch?

Most streamers start on Streamlabs because it's easy, then graduate to OBS Studio when they need more performance or advanced features. It is the natural lifecycle of a streamer.
