Quick Settings (TL;DR)

✅ Use these for most setups (RTX 3060 and above):
  • Encoder: NVENC H.264 (NVIDIA) or AMF H.264 (AMD)
  • Rate Control: CBR
  • Bitrate: 6000–8000 Kbps (Twitch) / 9000–12000 Kbps (YouTube)
  • Resolution: 1920×1080
  • FPS: 60
  • Preset: P5 (Quality)
  • Keyframe: 2 seconds
  • In-game: Enable DLSS Quality (NVIDIA) or FSR Quality (AMD)

Why Subnautica 2 Is Hard to Stream

Unlike the original Subnautica (which ran on Unity), Subnautica 2 uses Unreal Engine 5. This brings three major streaming challenges:

1. Lumen Global Illumination

Lumen is UE5's real-time global illumination system. It dynamically calculates how light bounces off every surface — beautiful, but massively GPU-intensive. In Subnautica 2's underwater environments, Lumen is calculating how bioluminescent creatures and thermal vents light up the water column in real time. This can push GPU usage to 95–100%, leaving virtually no headroom for OBS to encode.

2. Shader Compilation Stutter

On first launch (or after a driver update), Subnautica 2 compiles its shaders in the background. During this process, you will see stuttering regardless of your hardware — this is completely normal. Let the game run on the main menu for 5–10 minutes before going live. The stutter will stop once compilation is complete.

3. VRAM Pressure

Nanite geometry and high-resolution textures are memory-hungry. If you have an 8GB GPU and set textures to High or Ultra, you will overflow your VRAM — causing severe stuttering as assets stream from system RAM. Set texture quality to Medium on 8GB GPUs.

Troubleshooting: Stutter and Dropped Frames

Problem: Dropped frames in OBS

Fix: This means your GPU cannot handle the game + encoding at the same time. Enable DLSS/FSR Quality in-game, lower Global Illumination to Medium, and ensure you are using NVENC/AMF (not x264). If still dropping, reduce your output resolution to 1280×720.

Problem: Encoding overloaded warning in OBS

Fix: Change your NVENC preset from P5 to P4 (Medium). This reduces encoding quality slightly but takes less GPU time. Also lower your in-game graphics settings to free up GPU bandwidth.

Problem: Stream stutters during biome transitions

Fix: This is VRAM overflow. Set Texture Quality to Medium (for 8GB GPUs) or Low (for 6GB GPUs). The game streams new assets into VRAM when you enter a new biome area — if VRAM is full, it spills into system RAM and causes hitching.

Problem: Stutter only in the first 10 minutes

Fix: This is normal shader compilation. Subnautica 2 (like all UE5 games) compiles shaders on first launch. Let the game sit on the main menu for 5–10 minutes before going live. The stutter will stop permanently.

Problem: OBS black screen when capturing Subnautica 2

Fix: Right-click the game capture source in OBS and select "Recapture." If that does not work, switch from Game Capture to Window Capture. Alternatively, run OBS as administrator. For borderless/windowed mode players, switch the game to Windowed mode, then add it as a Window Capture source.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best OBS settings for Subnautica 2?

NVENC H.264 encoder, 6000–8000 Kbps CBR, 1080p 60fps, P5 preset, 2-second keyframe interval. Enable DLSS Quality or FSR Quality in-game to give OBS the GPU headroom it needs.

Why does Subnautica 2 stutter when I stream?

Unreal Engine 5's Lumen global illumination pushes GPUs to their limits. Enable DLSS/FSR Quality in-game, lower Global Illumination to Medium, cap your in-game FPS at 120, and always use hardware encoding (NVENC/AMF) — never x264.

Should I use NVENC or x264 for Subnautica 2?

Always NVENC (NVIDIA) or AMF (AMD). Subnautica 2 uses too much GPU for x264 to be viable. Hardware encoders use a dedicated chip on your GPU, so they do not compete with the game for resources.

What bitrate should I use for Subnautica 2 on Twitch?

Use 6000–8000 Kbps. Subnautica 2's dark underwater environments and particle effects compress poorly at low bitrates, causing visible blocky artifacts. Higher bitrate = cleaner stream in complex scenes.

Can I stream Subnautica 2 at 1440p?

Only if you have an RTX 4070 Ti or higher (NVIDIA) or RX 7900 XTX (AMD). At 1440p, your bitrate needs to be at least 12,000 Kbps — which is only practical on YouTube, not Twitch.
