Twitch Sub Badge Size Guide 2026

January 22, 2026 • 4 min read

Subscriber Badges (or Loyalty Badges) are the ultimate status symbol in your chat. They appear next to a user's name to show how long they have supported you. Because they are tiny, getting the size right is critical.

📏 Required Dimensions

Unlike emotes, badges do not have an auto-resize feature (yet). You must upload three exact sizes:

  • 18 × 18 px (Small - Chat)
  • 36 × 36 px (Medium - Retina)
  • 72 × 72 px (Large - Preview)

Format: PNG (Transparent) only. No GIFs allowed for badges.

Why 18px is Hard

18 pixels is incredibly small. Most beginners design a detailed badge at 500px and shrink it down, resulting in a blurry smudge.

Design Rule #1: Design for the 18px version first. If it looks good at 18px, it will look great at 72px. If you start big, you will fail.

Good Badge Ideas

  • Simple Shapes: Stars, Hearts, Gems, Coins.
  • High Contrast: Use bright colors with a dark outline.
  • Evolution: Change the color (Bronze > Silver > Gold) or add detail (Egg > Cracked Egg > Dragon) as months go by.

Standard Tier Milestones

Twitch allows you to convert standard badges to "Loyalty Badges" that update automatically:

  • Base (New Sub)
  • 2 Months
  • 3 Months
  • 6 Months
  • 9 Months
  • 1 Year

Don't feel pressured to fill every slot immediately. Start with a Base badge and a 6-Month badge.
