How to Fix "Encoding Overloaded" in OBS Studio (2026)

January 22, 2026 • 6 min read

It's the nightmare of every streamer: you're in the middle of a clutch play, and suddenly the message "Encoding Overloaded! Consider turning down video settings or using a faster encoding preset" appears in the status bar. Your stream turns into a slideshow. Here is exactly how to fix it.

🚨 What Does This Error Mean?

It means your computer cannot encode the video fast enough to keep up with the frame rate. If you are streaming at 60 FPS, your PC has 16.6 milliseconds to encode each frame. If it takes longer, OBS drops the frame, causing lag.

Solution 1: Switch to Hardware Encoding (NVENC/AMF)

This is the #1 fix for 90% of users. By default, OBS might be using x264 (CPU encoding). This crushes your processor while you are trying to game.

How to fix:

  1. Go to Settings > Output.
  2. Change "Output Mode" to Advanced.
  3. For "Video Encoder", select NVIDIA NVENC H.264 (if you have an NVIDIA GPU) or AMD HW H.264 (for AMD).
  4. Click Apply.

Why this works: It offloads the work to a dedicated chip on your graphics card, freeing up your CPU for the game.

Solution 2: Cap Your In-Game FPS

Even if you use NVENC, OBS needs a tiny bit of GPU power to compose the scene (put the webcam over the game). If your game is using 100% of your GPU, OBS gets choked out.

The Fix: If you have a 144Hz monitor, cap your game FPS to 144 (or even 120). Do not let it run "Uncapped".

Run OBS as Administrator. This tells Windows to give OBS priority over the game ensuring it gets the GPU resources it needs.

Solution 3: Lower Output Resolution

Encoding 1080p requires processing over 2 million pixels per frame. 720p is less than 1 million. Dropping to 720p cuts the workload in half.

  1. Go to Settings > Video.
  2. Keep "Base Resolution" at your monitor size (e.g., 1920x1080).
  3. Change "Output (Scaled) Resolution" to 1280x720.
  4. Set "Downscale Filter" to Lanczos for sharpness.

💡 Pro Tip: 60 FPS vs 30 FPS

If you are still lagging at 720p, drop your frame rate from 60 to 30 FPS. This instantly reduces the encoding load by exactly 50%.

Solution 4: Check for "Heavy" Sources

Sometimes a single browser source or filter is the culprit.

  • Browser Sources: Turn off "Shutdown source when not visible" for animated overlays to prevent lag spikes when switching scenes, BUT ensure "Hardware Acceleration" is enabled in OBS Advanced settings.
  • Filters: "Blur" and "Color Correction" filters are GPU intensive. Use them sparingly.

Solution 5: Changing the Preset (x264 Users Only)

If you must use CPU encoding (x264) because you have no GPU:

  1. Go to Settings > Output.
  2. Find "CPU Usage Preset". Default is usually veryfast.
  3. Change it to superfast or ultrafast.

Warning: This will make the video look blocky/pixelated (lower quality), but it will stop the stuttering.

Summary Checklist

  • ✅ Use NVENC or AMD, not x264.
  • ✅ Run OBS as Administrator.
  • ✅ Cap in-game FPS (leave room for OBS).
  • ✅ Lower Output Resolution to 720p if needed.
  • ✅ Use Game Capture instead of Display Capture (it's faster).
