Twitch Banner Size Guide 2026: Profile, Offline & Video Player

January 20, 2026 • 5 min read

Your Twitch channel has three main "banners," and getting them mixed up is a common rookie mistake. A pixelated or cropped banner screams "amateur." This guide covers the exact dimensions for every graphical asset you need.

📏 Quick Cheat Sheet (2026)

  • Profile Banner: 1200 × 480 pixels (Top of channel page)
  • Offline Screen: 1920 × 1080 pixels (When you are offline)
  • Video Player Banner: 1920 × 1080 pixels (Same as offline screen)
  • Profile Picture (Avatar): 256 × 256 pixels

1. Profile Banner (1200 x 480px)

This is the graphic that appears at the very top of your channel page behind your profile picture and video player area. It is vital for branding.

The "Safe Zone" Problem

Twitch scales this image differently on mobile, desktop, and ultrawide monitors. The bottom-left and bottom-center are usually covered by your Avatar and Stream Title.

Design Tip: Keep your logo, text, and important details in the center-right or top-center of the image. Avoid putting text in the bottom 30% of the image.

Max File Size: 10MB (JPEG, PNG, GIF).

2. Offline Screen (1920 x 1080px)

Also known as the "Video Player Banner," this is what viewers see when they visit your channel while you are offline. It is a massive 1080p canvas that acts as a billboard.

What to Include:

  • Schedule: "Live Mon-Fri @ 5PM EST"
  • Socials: Twitter/X, Instagram, YouTube handles
  • Call to Action: "Follow to get notified when I go live!"
  • Discord Link: A short, memorable link (e.g. discord.gg/yourname)

3. Video Player Banner

Confusingly, Twitch sometimes refers to the Offline Screen as the "Video Player Banner." They are effectively the same spot. Upload your 1920x1080 offline screen here in your Creator Dashboard under Settings > Channel > Brand.

Design Best Practices for 2026

Consistency is King

Use the same color palette (hex codes) and fonts across your Profile Banner, Offline Screen, and Panels. If your Offline Screen is dark mode neon but your Profile Banner is bright pastel, it feels disjointed.

Less is More

Don't clutter your banners with 50 different game characters. Pick 1-2 key visual elements (your mascot or logo) and use negative space to make the text readable.

Mobile Responsiveness

Over 50% of Twitch viewers watch on mobile. Your elaborate 1200px wide Profile Banner will be crunched down on a phone screen. Always test your banners on the Twitch mobile app after uploading.
