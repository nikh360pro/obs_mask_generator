Twitch Panel Size Guide 2026: Best Dimensions & Formats

January 20, 2026 • 4 min read

Twitch panels are the backbone of your channel page. They direct viewers to your donation page, Discord server, and social media. But if your panels are the wrong size, they will look blurry, stretched, or unprofessional.

📏 The Golden Numbers for 2026

  • Width: Exactly 320 pixels (Required)
  • Height: Flexible (Recommended 75px - 150px)
  • Max File Size: 2.9 MB per panel
  • Format: PNG (Best quality) or GIF (Animation)

Why 320px Width Matters

Twitch's interface is hard-coded to display panels at 320 pixels wide. If you design a panel that is 600px wide, Twitch will automatically shrink it down to 320px. This resizing process often makes text look blurry or "crunchy".

Pro Tip: Always design your canvas at exactly 320px width to ensure what you see is what you get.

Height Best Practices

While width is fixed, height is flexible. However, consistency is key:

  • Standard Headers: 320px × 100px (Most popular)
  • Tall Info Cards: 320px × 300px (Great for "Rules" or "PC Specs")
  • Small Buttons: 320px × 50px (Good for social links)

File Formats: PNG vs. JPG vs. GIF

Format Best For Pros Cons
PNG Most Panels Supports transparency, sharp text, no compression artifacts. Larger file size than JPG.
GIF Animated Panels Allows movement (glow, sparkle, bounce). Limited to 256 colors, creating "banding" on gradients.
JPG Photos Smallest file size. No transparency (white corners), compression artifacts on text.

Verdict: Always use PNG unless you specifically need animation. Never use JPG for graphics with text.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

1. "My transparent corners have a white background"

This happens if you save your file as a JPG. JPGs do not support alpha channels (transparency). Re-save your file as a PNG-24.

2. "My text looks blurry on Twitch"

This is usually caused by uploading a large image (e.g., 1920px wide) and letting Twitch resize it. Resize your image to 320px width on your computer before uploading.

3. "File size too large" error

Twitch has a strict 2.9 MB limit per panel. This is common with animated GIFs. To fix this:

  • Reduce the number of frames in your GIF
  • Reduce the height of the panel
  • Use a simpler color palette
