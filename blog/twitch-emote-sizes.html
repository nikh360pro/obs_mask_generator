Twitch Emote Size Guide 2026: Auto-Resize vs Manual

January 20, 2026 • 5 min read

Emotes are the currency of Twitch culture. But uploading them used to be a pain, requiring three different resized versions. In 2026, Twitch's "Auto-Resize" feature has changed the game.

🚀 The Only Size You Need (Auto-Resize)

For standard static emotes, you only need to upload one file:

  • Dimensions: Between 112 × 112 px and 4096 × 4096 px
  • Shape: Square (Aspect Ratio 1:1)
  • File Size: Under 1 MB
  • Format: PNG (Transparent background)

Manual Mode (The Old Way)

If you prefer to disable Auto-Resize to manually control the pixel perfection at small sizes, you must upload three specific sizes:

  1. 28 × 28 px (Web Chat)
  2. 56 × 56 px (Retina Display / Mobile)
  3. 112 × 112 px (Large Preview)

Why do manual? Sometimes Auto-Resize makes pixel art look blurry. For pixel art emotes, we recommend manually resizing using "Nearest Neighbor" scaling in Photoshop to keep the pixels crisp.

Animated Emotes

Animated emotes are available to all Affiliates and Partners. The requirements are stricter:

  • Format: GIF only
  • File Size: Under 1 MB (Strict!)
  • Dimensions: Auto-Resize works here too! Upload a single square GIF between 112x112 and 4096x4096px.
  • Frames: Max 60 frames per second.

⚠️ No fast blinking!

Twitch will reject animated emotes with flashing lights or rapid strobing (epilepsy safety). Keep animations smooth and slow.

Follower Emotes

Follower emotes allow viewers to use your emotes without subscribing. They have the same size requirements as subscriber emotes but are often restricted to static PNGs depending on your Partner status.
