The Ultimate Guide to OBS Webcam Masks

If you're looking to upgrade your stream's visual appeal, one of the easiest and most effective changes you can make is applying a webcam mask. Gone are the days of the boring, standard 16:9 rectangular camera feed that clutters up your scene. In this comprehensive guide, we'll explore everything you need to know about OBS webcam masks—from what they are to how you can create and apply them to give your stream a professional polish.

What is an OBS Webcam Mask?

An OBS Webcam Mask (or Image Mask) is a black-and-white image file that tells OBS Studio which parts of your webcam to show and which parts to hide. It works on a simple principle: White reveals, and Black conceals. Gray areas create transparency.

By applying a mask filter to your video capture device, you can change your camera shape from a rectangle to a circle, a rounded square, a hexagon, or any custom shape you can imagine—without needing a green screen.

Key Benefits of Using a Mask:

  • Saves Screen Real Estate: Cropping unnecessary background clutter allows your gameplay or content to take center stage.
  • Professional Branding: Unique shapes like hexagons or squircles can match your channel's overlay theme.
  • Clean Look: Rounded corners feel more modern and polished than sharp, harsh edges.
  • No Performance Cost: Unlike complex shaders, static image masks use virtually zero CPU/GPU resources.

Top 5 Popular Webcam Shapes for Streamers

Choosing the right shape depends on your layout and personal style. Here are the most popular choices used by top streamers on Twitch and YouTube:

1. The Circle (Circular Mask)

The classic circle is perfect for "Just Chatting" scenes or minimal gameplay intermissions. It focuses entirely on your face and removes distracting background elements like messy rooms or doors. It's often used by VTubers and speedrunners who need a compact camera footprint.

2. The Rounded Square (Squircle)

A "squircle" or rounded square is the modern standard. It preserves more of your camera's field of view than a circle but eliminates the sharp, aggressive corners of a standard rectangle. It fits seamlessly into almost any overlay.

3. The Hexagon

Hexagons are a favorite among sci-fi, tech, and FPS streamers. The angular geometry communicates a "gamer" aesthetic and tiles well with other UI elements like mini-maps or health bars.

4. The Vertical Portrait (9:16)

With the rise of TikTok and YouTube Shorts, many streamers are opting for a vertical crop. This is ideal for repurposing clips for social media without needing to re-crop your camera later.

5. Custom Shapes

The sky's the limit! You can use hearts for cozy streams, brush-stroke edges for art streams, or even your logo's silhouette.

How to Add a Webcam Mask in OBS Studio (Step-by-Step)

Adding a mask is incredibly simple in OBS Studio using the native "Image Mask/Blend" filter. Follow these steps:

  1. Create or Download a Mask: Use our free OBS Mask Generator tool above to create your perfect shape. Adjust the corner radius, border width, and aspect ratio, then click "Download Mask".
  2. Open OBS Studio: Launch OBS and go to your "Sources" dock.
  3. Select Your Camera: Right-click on your webcam source (usually named "Video Capture Device") and select "Filters".
  4. Add the Filter: In the "Effect Filters" section (bottom half), click the + (Plus) icon and choose "Image Mask/Blend". Give it a name like "Circle Mask".
  5. Configure Settings:
    • Type: Select "Alpha Mask (Alpha Channel)" if your PNG has transparency, or "Alpha Mask (Color Channel)" if it's a black/white JPG. Our generator produces PNGs, so generally, "Alpha Mask (Alpha Channel)" is the safest bet, but if it looks wrong, try "Alpha Mask (Luminance)".
    • Path: Click "Browse" and select the mask file you just downloaded.
  6. Close and Verify: Click "Close". Your webcam should now be shaped according to the mask!

Pro Tip: Adding a Border

A mask hides part of your video, but it doesn't add a colored outline. To add a border:

  1. In our generator, click "Download Border Overlay" after creating your mask.
  2. In OBS, add a new "Image" source above your webcam source.
  3. Select the border file.
  4. It should align perfectly on top of your masked camera!

Troubleshooting Common Issues

My Mask looks inverted or weird!

If your camera looks ghostly or the wrong parts are transparent, go back to the Filter settings in OBS. Change the "Type" dropdown. Usually, switching from "Alpha Mask (Alpha Channel)" to "Alpha Mask (Luminance)" or vice versa fixes this immediately.

The Mask is stretching or squishing my camera

This happens if the aspect ratio of your mask doesn't match your camera. For example, applying a 1:1 square mask to a 16:9 widescreen camera will stretch the circle into an oval. Ensure your mask resolution (e.g., 1920x1080) matches your camera's base resolution.
