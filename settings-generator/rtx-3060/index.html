Why These Settings Work for RTX 3060

The NVIDIA GeForce RTX 3060 remains one of the most popular GPUs for streamers in 2026, thanks to its 7th generation NVENC encoder and generous 12GB VRAM. While it lacks AV1 support, the H.264 encoding quality is excellent and compatible with all platforms.

The 12GB VRAM is a significant advantage over the RTX 4060 (8GB), allowing you to stream while playing more graphically demanding games without VRAM bottlenecks.

The P6 preset provides the best quality-to-performance ratio for the RTX 3060. The single encoder means you're limited to one simultaneous encode, but that's sufficient for most streamers.

Common Issues with RTX 3060 Streaming

  • No AV1 Support: Unlike RTX 40 series, the RTX 3060 cannot encode AV1. Stick to H.264 for maximum compatibility.
  • Single Encoder: Cannot multi-stream to different platforms at different quality settings simultaneously.
  • Aging Architecture: While still capable, consider upgrading to RTX 4060 for AV1 support if you primarily stream to YouTube.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is RTX 3060 good for streaming?

Yes, the RTX 3060 is still excellent for 1080p streaming in 2026. The 7th gen NVENC provides great quality, and the 12GB VRAM gives you headroom for demanding games.

Does RTX 3060 support AV1 encoding?

No. Hardware AV1 encoding is only available on RTX 40 series (Ada Lovelace) and newer. RTX 3060 supports H.264 and HEVC only.

What bitrate for RTX 3060 Twitch?

Use 6000 Kbps (CBR) for 1080p 60fps Twitch streaming. This is Twitch's maximum and provides excellent quality with NVENC H.264.
