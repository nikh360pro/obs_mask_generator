The Ultimate Guide to OBS Studio Hotkeys

Streaming requires multitasking. You are playing a game, reading chat, and managing your broadcast all at once. Fumbling with your mouse to switch scenes or mute your mic looks unprofessional. The solution? Hotkeys.

Why You Need Hotkeys

Hotkeys (keyboard shortcuts) allow you to control OBS without leaving your game window. They are the poor man's Stream Deck. Instead of $150 hardware, you just use the keys you already have.

Essential Hotkeys Every Streamer Needs

  • Switch Scenes (BRB / Game / Chatting): Instantly cut away if you need to sneeze or answer the door.
  • Mute Mic (Toggle): Crucial for coughs or private conversations.
  • Save Replay Buffer: If you hit a cool clip, press a key to save the last 60 seconds of gameplay to your hard drive immediately.

How to Set Hotkeys in OBS

  1. Open OBS Studio.
  2. Go to Settings > Hotkeys.
  3. Scroll to the action you want (e.g., "Start Recording").
  4. Click the empty box and press your desired key combination (e.g., Ctrl + Shift + R).
  5. Click Apply.

Best Practices

Avoid using single keys like "M" for mute, as you will accidentally trigger it while typing in chat. Always use combinations like Ctrl + Shift + Key or Alt + Key to prevent accidents.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I find hotkeys in OBS?

Go to Settings > Hotkeys. You can use the search bar at the top to find specific functions like 'Studio Mode' or 'Start Recording'.

Why are my OBS hotkeys not working?

If hotkeys don't work while you are gaming, try running OBS as Administrator (Right-click icon > Run as Administrator). This gives OBS priority to listen to key presses.

Can I use a controller for hotkeys?

OBS natively supports keyboard hotkeys. For game controllers or MIDI devices, you might need third-party plugins or mapping software like JoyToKey.
