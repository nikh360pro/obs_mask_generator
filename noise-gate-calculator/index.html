Frequently Asked Questions

What is a noise gate in OBS?

A noise gate is an audio filter that silences your microphone when the audio level drops below a certain threshold. This removes background noise like keyboard clicks, fans, and ambient sounds when you're not speaking.

How do I find my noise floor in OBS?

In OBS, add your microphone as an audio source, then remain silent and watch the audio mixer. The level where the meter consistently peaks while you're quiet is your noise floor - typically between -60dB and -40dB.

What are the best OBS noise gate settings?

Set your Close Threshold about 5dB below your noise floor, and Open Threshold about 5-8dB above it. Use 6ms attack, 100ms hold, and 60-100ms release for natural-sounding results.

Should I use noise gate or noise suppression?

Use both! Noise suppression (like RNNoise) reduces constant background noise, while noise gate cuts audio completely when you're not speaking. Apply noise suppression first, then noise gate.
