How to Make a Professional Twitch Starting Screen (Free Tool)

Your "Starting Soon" screen is the first thing viewers see. It sets the mood, lets early birds chat, and gives you a few minutes to check your audio levels before going live. But you don't need Photoshop to make one.

Why You Need a Starting Screen

  • Builds Hype: A simple 5-10 minute countdown builds anticipation.
  • Tech Check: Gives you a buffer to ensure your stream is stable and audio is synced.
  • Chat Engagement: Allows your community to gather and say hello before the show starts.

How to Use This Generator

  1. Customize Text: Change "Starting Soon" to your own brand message, like "The Show Begins In..." or your channel name.
  2. Pick Your Colors: Match the background and text colors to your Twitch channel's theme.
  3. Add a Countdown: While this tool creates the static background, we recommend adding a Countdown Timer layer on top in OBS.
  4. Download & Add to OBS: Click "Download Image", then add it as an Image Source in OBS Studio.

Pro Tip: Add Background Music

A silent starting screen is awkward. Add a "Media Source" in OBS with some copyright-free Lo-Fi or synthwave music to vibe with your visual. Keep the volume lower than your voice (around -20dB to -25dB).

Best Practices

Keep it simple. You don't need clutter. Your social media handles (which you can add with our Overlay Generator) and a clear title are enough. The focus should be on the chat and the music.

How to Use

Adding to OBS

Download the PNG for a static image, or HTML for animated effects. In OBS, add an Image source (for PNG) or Browser source (for HTML) and set to 1920×1080.

When to use Starting Soon screens

Display your Starting Soon screen while setting up before going live. It lets early viewers know you'll be starting shortly and looks more professional than a blank screen.
